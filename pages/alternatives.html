Alternatives to Plastic

Plastic is a very useful material that was made for certain occasion. However, our dependance on plastic has caused many downsides to the human health and the environment too. Therefore, corporations have researched alternatives to plastic. They include recyclable materials such as-

Glass

Glass, the previous cornerstone of packaging drinks have become mostly irrelevant in the 21st century. Nowadays, if you try to buy drinks at stores or even store them at home, we use plastic. Although plastic has the properties to be durable and not break easily unlike glass, plastic which is derived from fossil fuels hurts the environment much more than we can think of. Therefore, substituing plastic with glass is a good choice while being made from sand meaning it is also recyclable and reusable once washed. Plastics also offer the danger of breaking into microplastics in hot temperatures which could potentially leach your food with unedible bits of material that your body can't digest well. However, glass offers 100% purity with no loss in quality.

Liquid Wood

Liquid Wood is a material made by combining the cells of living organisms also known as a biopolymer material which is also an alternative to plastic and other syntehtic materials. Liquid wood possesses almost smiliar traits to plastic but is a sustainable version due to it being biodegradable, recyclable and coming from renewable sources. This material has also been applied in various facilities such as manufacturing, packaging, etc. Additionally, liquid wood is a waterproof material once dry meaning that it can be used underwater. It also has other properties such as being non-toxic, lightweight, strong and flexible which helps humans create products with it.

Bamboo

Although bamboo comes from plants which can account to deforestation, bamboo is widely considered a sustainable material due to its' rapid growth, low agricultural requirements and minimum harm to the planet which makes the production of bamboo efficient to harvest while having high produce of it to continue biodiversity. Bamboo is also a recycable material making it quite popular in the current market with products such as human necessities to product packaging.

Where are these materials used?

Glass

Glass is normally used as a storing apparatus. Hence, we can identify the use of glass in kitchenware as an alternative to the usages of plastic.

  1. Tubwares, cups and jugs are generally used with glass as it is more environmentally friendly.
  2. Glass is a transparent material, it is used in the creation of glasses, windows or even to scatter light in lighting systems.
  3. During lab experiments, glass is also used to store harsh chemicals and to allow microscopes to work.

Liquid Wood

Liquid wood is generally a durable material that can compete against the properties of plastic. Thus, it is generally used in manufacturing.

  1. Liquid wood is used to glue materials or items together underwater or as a substance to stick items to a surface such as mounting mirrors to a wall or fixing cracked pots.
  2. It is also used as the framework for creating cabinets, door panels and other furniture assets.

Bamboo

Bamboo is a universal product used in everyday life.

  1. It is used to create bamboo toothbrushes, tableware, straws, packages and kitchen products.
  2. It evens play a huge role in replacing plastic for construction.
  3. It is also used as decoration, flooring plans, medicine, cultural arts or eaten as food.
